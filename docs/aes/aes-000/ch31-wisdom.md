# AES-000 Ch 31 — Educational Wisdom (`public/aquin-wisdom.js`)  — Part I capstone

Knowledge=WHAT, intelligence=HOW, **wisdom=WHETHER/WHEN**. Berlin Wisdom Paradigm
(Baltes & Staudinger): judgment under uncertainty. Node-tested. Principles:
- **W1 Precaution** — uncertain + high-stakes + irreversible ⇒ wait / defer, never act boldly.
- **W2 Reversibility bias** — when uncertain, prefer the undoable option.
- **W3 Temporal** — long-term learning outranks short-term gain (usage ≠ learning).
- **W4 Competence humility** — outside competence ⇒ defer to human.
- **W5 Value alignment** — violates dignity/values ⇒ refuse, route to human/ethics.
Verdicts: act · proceed-with-caution · choose-reversible · wait-gather-evidence ·
defer-to-human · refuse — always acknowledging its own uncertainty. Composes
Meta-Cognition (Ch 56) + Ethics (Ch 62).
